Pantala case study

1. Introduction

Pantala aims to make traveling less of a hassle for hardworking parents in need of family time, away from work time, self-care time, or all of the above.

2. Research phase

Potential users were surveyed on their travel habits. Recurring themes included emphasis on family activities, organization, and affordability.

3. Problem statement

This case study aims to address the convoluted and inefficient travel booking process in a way that is accessible to parents and doesn't break the bank.

4. Ideation phase

Features added during ideation include an interactive map, saved favorites feature, an itinerary feature with opt-in alert capability, a community chat, and a budget tool.

5. Prototyping phase

During the prototyping phase, colors were added to generate excitement and exude sophistication. The onboarding process was mapped out as well.

6. Conclusion

Future development will require consistently updated listings for lodging, eateries, and activities.
